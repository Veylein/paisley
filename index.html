Ainu people live in Hokkaido, Sakhalin, and the Kuril Island. They like environments like coastline, forest, and river, where they hunted, fished, gathered, and cultivated land. The Ainu built houses called chise that were made of natural materials such as trees, bamboo grass, or reeds, tied together with plants such as grape vines. The four-sided roofs were slanted down to allow snow to slide off, and vents at the top enabled smoke from the hearth to escape. A separate raised structure for food storage was close to the chise.

Ainu cuisine is characterized by its reliance on hunting, gathering, and fishing, with a focus on seasonal and regional ingredients. Traditional Ainu dishes often feature salmon, deer, bear, and various wild plants and berries. Ohaw (soup) and rataskep (simmered dish) are central to Ainu food culture. Their special environment not only affects their food, but it also affects their clothing. The matanpushi (Ainu/Japanese: マタンプシ) is a traditional garment. The matanpushi is a traditional, embroidered headband worn by Ainu women, particularly during ceremonies. While originally worn by both men and women, it's now primarily associated with women, complementing the sapanpe, a crown worn by men in Ainu ceremonies.

The Ainu people, an indigenous group in Japan, have rich traditions and celebrations deeply connected to their reverence for spirits includes traditional dances, rituals for sending off animal spirits, and festivals centered around offerings and prayers for a good harvest or safe sea voyages. In Ainu men primarily responsible for hunting and fishing, while women focused on gathering plants, preparing food, and making clothing and mats.

If you are amoung the Ainu, you will be immersed in a culture deeply connected to nature. Traditional Ainu communities relied on hunting, fishing, and gathering, with a strong spiritual belief system rooted in animism—the idea that all things, from animals to rivers, have spirits. You might participate in ceremonies honoring bears or salmon, which are considered sacred.
